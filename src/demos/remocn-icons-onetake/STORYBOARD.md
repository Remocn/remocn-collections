# STORYBOARD — Introducing remocn icons (one-take cut)

The second cut of the remocn icons announcement. The eight scenes are the
main cut's scenes, unchanged — same copy, same beats, same anti-slop rules
(see `../remocn-icons/STORYBOARD.md` for the per-scene breakdown). What
changes is the grammar between them: the entire transition system is removed
and replaced by ONE continuous camera drive across a monitor wall.

- **The rig:** the whole video is one huge flat wall. All eight scenes stand
  side by side as 1280×720 tiles with a 150px gap, laid out as a snake —
  right, right, down · left, left, down · right. Nothing fades, nothing
  reveals: every scene is always there.
- **The camera:** sits perfectly still on a tile while its scene plays
  (zoom exactly 1 — pixel-crisp, zero shimmer), then pulls back to 0.62,
  glides to the next tile on an eased 28-frame move and settles in. Pan and
  zoom both have zero velocity at every joint, so the drive never judders.
- **The wall is alive:** each scene starts playing 34 frames before the
  camera lands — mid-glide the next monitor is already waking up — and
  finished scenes keep standing at their last frame, so every pull-back
  shows the previous video resting beside the next one running. The two
  hook lines even share a frame mid-glide: "Everything in your video moves"
  frozen on the left, "Except the icons" dead-still on the right.
- **The field:** the neuro-noise web stays pinned to the lens — the wall
  slides past over it. A whisper of screen-speed motion blur rides the
  glides only.
- **Length:** ~45.5s @ 30fps (1366 frames) — travel time is part of the
  language.
